ISAAC ASIMOV

Isaac Asimov was born January 2 1920 in Petrovichi Russia. He moved to the americas at the age of three. He grew up in Brooklyn, New York and graduated from Columbia University with a degree in chemistry in 1939.

First short story

In 1939 Asimov got his first short story called Marooned off Vesta put in the daily newspaper and he then sold it to a company called Amazing stories.

First short story series

In 1940 he started his robot series. This series was one of his first series he ever made.

Asimov's best series

In 1942 Asimov made his most popular series called the Foundation. He kicked it of with a story called the Encyclopediast.

Asimov's first novels

His first novels were made between 1950 and 1952. The novels are called Pebble in the Sky, The Stars, Like Dust, and The Currents of Space.

Asimov pioneered the idea of modern science fiction. If it wasn't for Asimov, we wouldn't have all the cool science fiction movie and story franchises we have now. He literally created the word “Robotics”. Everything around us may have been different if it wasn't for Asimov. He is a man that should be respected and thanked.

Isaac Asimov passed away on April 6, 1992 in New York, New York. He had contributed a lot to society and the science fiction world. It's sad to see him go, but it was bound to happen. He had heart and kidney failure witch was the only failure he had in his life. He had works ranging from preschool to college level. He was a true legend and we owe him our gratitude.
